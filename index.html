Bio



Tao Long is a college senior studying Information Science and Communication at Cornell University. Advised by Dr. François Guimbretière and Dr. Katherine Sender, Supporting users' online behaviors and interactions in a scalable and accessible way, Tao's works falls into the beautiful field of Human-Computer Interaction(HCI).

Through a human-centered lens, Tao’s research aims to understand how to design and build (computer-mediated / AI-mediated / XR-mediated) technical and social supports for collaborative environments and marginalized communities. Please go to the Projects sections to see Tao's works.

Projects



Tao has been contributing to and leading multiple research projects in both collaboration-oriented and accessibility-oriented settings of immigrant affairs, teaching & learning, multicultural communication, workspace relationships, social media, and digital/wearable health tech.

Asylum Seeker's Tech

Advisor: Dr. Natalie Bazarova


Design and prototype an online web tool that addresses the asylum seekers’ information asymmetry problem by helping gather and present correct legal and healthcare information

Virtual Cyberball Task

Advisor: Dr. Andrea Won


Design and build a virtual Cyberball task for social science research and examine how customizable avatars impact the Cyberball while testing interpersonal ostracism and exclusion

Immersive Remote Debugging

Advisor: Dr. François Guimbretière


Lead the virtual reality interface design and implementation subproject for a joint-debugging telepresence product RemoteCode for the preliminary scenario of breadboard testing

SenseTrans

Advisor: Dr. Susan Fussell


Develop a social media plug-in application that translates emotional and contextual knowledge of foreign language media posts to avoid potential misunderstandings

Technology Solution for the Informal Organizational Communication Loss Under COVID

Advisor: Dr. Susan Fussell


Investigate the shifts of the informal communication in organizations under the pandemic and related technology accommodations

Changing Table

Advisor: Dr. Victor Paúl Pauca


Design and prototype a portable, foldable, affordable, easy-to-clean diaper-changing table for youths with disabilities, which aids their caregivers in changing the youths’ diapers without putting them on the floor of public restrooms
