Hi, I'm a first-year Computer Science PhD student at Columbia University, with a focus on human-computer interaction (HCI). I'm fortunate to be advised by Dr. Lydia Chilton and part of the Computational Design Lab.

My research focuses on making generative AI models more usable, useful, and well-integrated into our daily workflows. Thus, I build multimodal AI productivity & creativity support tools (yeee!!) Specifically, I am researching the human-AI interaction design with large language models (LLMs) and text-to-image models in the context of workspaces. For more, see my CV :)

I earned my bachelors with Summa Cum Laude from Cornell University, where I conducted research with Dr. Andrea Won, Dr. Natalie Bazarova, and Dr. Susan Fussell. I also TA-ed a lot for Dr. Gilly Leshed and Dr. Kyle Harms

Research

My Info/Sci and Communication background made me understand that technology has both communicative capacity and responsibility. Therefore, as designers and developers, we should speak, design, and build for those who can't. As a PhD newbie, I am also more than eager to learn and grow by joining exciting HCI/AI projects. Don't hesitate to reach out for collaboration ;)

Tweetorial Hooks: Generative AI Tools to Motivate Science on Social Media
Tao Long, Dorothy Zhang, Grace Li, Batool Taraif, Samia Menon, Kynnedy Simone Smith, Sitong Wang, Katy Ilonka Gero, and Lydia B. Chilton.
ICCC 2023 [ paper / video / demo / code / project page ]

TL;DR: Effective communication of STEM is crucial in a rapidly changing world. Tweetorials are popular, but experts struggle to make them engaging. We propose using large language models (LLMs) to help them create relatable hooks and spark curiosity. Our evaluation shows this reduces cognitive load and motivates readers. LLMs can support contextualizing technical / science materials for public engagement.

Social Dynamics of AI Support in Creative Writing
Katy Ilonka Gero, Tao Long, and Lydia B. Chilton.
CHI 2023 [ paper / video / tweet / acm / press ]

TL;DR: Large language models (LLMs) have proven able to follow complicated instructions and generate coherent text, while less work considers the social-technical gap that this technology poses. We discover three elements that govern a writer‚Äôs interaction with support actors: 1) what writers desire help with, 2) how writers perceive potential support actors, and 3) the values writers hold. We align our results with existing frameworks and uncover the social dynamics between users and generative AI.

Diverging Perspectives from Naive Participants and Experienced Social Science Researchers on Addressing Embodiment in a Virtual Cyberball Task
Tao Long, Swati Pandita, and Andrea Stevenson Won.
CSCW 2023 [ extended abstract / code / acm / award 1 / award 2 ]

TL;DR: There is a lack of research on the application of user-centered design methodologies in non-HCI fields, particularly for designing social science paradigms. Thus, we identify shared considerations as well as disagreements between the naive participants and experienced researchers for future iterations of our prototype and for designing virtual paradigms for social science research in general. Themes include: 1) Design for intuitive use, 2) Design for inclusivity, and 3) Realism vs minimalism in design.

Multistakeholder Perspectives on Digital Tools for U.S. Asylum Applicants Seeking Healthcare and Legal Information
Aparajita Bhandari, Diana Freed, Tara Pilato, Faten Taki, Gunisha Kaur, Stephen Yale-Loehr, Jane Powers, Tao Long, and Natalie Bazarova.
CSCW 2022 [ paper / video / acm / press 1 / press 2 / press 3 / project page ]

TL;DR: A lack of clear public benefits information for asylum applicants negatively impacts their healthcare engagement, but digital tools offer a promising solution. We identify four challenges for immigrants using current digital tools: lack of information, inaccessibility, emotional barriers, and lack of cultural sensitivity. The study recommends considering multiple perspectives when designing new tools for immigration information and improving existing digital tools for asylum seekers and those who support them.

Generative Disco: Text-to-Video for Music Visualization
Vivian Liu, Tao Long, Nathan Raw, and Lydia B. Chilton.
Under Review :) [ prepint / video / demo / code / tweet ]

TL;DR: Visuals enhance our music experience, and generative AI models can explore the intersection of visuals, language, and music. Our system, Generative Disco, helps generate music visualizations using large language models (LLMs) and text-to-image models. The videos are generated with start and end prompts and design patterns. Professionals found the system intuitive to use and highly expressive. We discuss the system's potential beyond the use cases and how AI-generated content is changing creative work.
ReelFramer: Co-creating News Reels on Social Media with Generative AI
Sitong Wang, Samia Menon, Tao Long, Keren Henderson, Dingzeyu Li, Kevin Crowston, Mark Hansen, Jeffrey V. Nickerson, and Lydia B. Chilton.
Under Review :) [ prepint / video / code / tweet ]

TL;DR: Short videos on social media, such as TikTok and Instagram Reels, are popular. However, news outlets struggle to create news reels that match the style of these platforms. Our system ReelFramer, uses large language models (LLMs) and text-to-image models to help journalists explore multiple framings for a story and generate scripts, character boards, and storyboards. Our user study and co-design workshop found our system eases the burden of transforming written news stories and makes it rewarding.

Challenges and Opportunities for the Design of Smart Speakers
Tao Long and Lydia B. Chilton.
Under Submission :) [ preprint / video ]

TL;DR: Voice User Interfaces (VUIs) have opened up many new types of interaction, while they are still underused. We synthesize existing VUI design guidelines and distill four design challenges that contribute the most to users' non-use and technology abandonment. Then, we identify opportunity spaces for designers and developers to explore such as focusing on 1) information support while multitasking (cooking, driving, childcare, etc), 2) incorporating users‚Äô mental models, and 3) integrating calm design principles.
